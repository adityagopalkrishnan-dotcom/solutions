QuestionPro — Internal Ticketing & Support Process
====================================================
Source: Internal QP Wiki (other-departments/customers/operations/ticketing-systems)

TYPES OF TICKETS:

1. CLIENT ISSUE TICKET (Radar)
   Purpose: Track work done for the client — justifies subscription cost and provides transparency.
   Exposure: These tickets ARE visible to clients under "My Account" section.
   Use for: Any issue, feature gap, or task directly related to a client's experience.

2. INTERNAL INFORMATION TICKET (RE-Ticket)
   Purpose: Store and pass internal information between CSM and AM teams.
   Exposure: These are INTERNAL ONLY — not exposed to clients.
   Use for: Internal notes, crucial information that only the team needs.

3. BUG REPORTS
   Definition: When the product is not doing what it is advertised to do.
   Types: Feature issue, annoyance (too many clicks/too slow), UX issue, UI issue.
   Who reports: CSMs must report bugs of any magnitude to the product team.
   Timeline: Bugs requiring a build take at least 15 days to fix.
   How to report: Follow the "Report Bug" process document.

4. FEATURE REQUESTS
   Definition: Enhancements to existing functions/features or new capabilities.
   Process:
   1. Raise on IdeaScale first
   2. Get your team to upvote it
   3. If business-critical, escalate to team leader
   Timeline: Feature requests can take 1 month to 1 year if accepted for development.
   DO NOT PROMISE features to clients without a YouTrack (YT) ticket AND PDM confirmation.

TICKETING TOOL: Radar (admin.questionpro.com)
DEVELOPMENT TRACKING: YouTrack (internal)
PRODUCT IDEAS: IdeaScale

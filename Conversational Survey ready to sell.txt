1. What problem does this solution solve?

Traditional surveys capture a score but fail to capture the story behind it. Every respondent receives the same static follow-up, which creates shallow insight, inconsistent open-ended feedback and more manual analysis work for CX teams.

AI Router Conversational Surveys solve this by turning static survey flows into adaptive conversations that respond in real time, uncover root causes and structure qualitative feedback into actionable CX intelligence.

2. What is the solution and how does it work?

What it does

AI Router transforms a standard survey into an intelligent conversational experience. Each response is analyzed in real time to detect sentiment, themes, intensity and intent, allowing the system to decide the most relevant next question dynamically.

How it fits into the existing product

The solution is built inside QuestionPro CX and QuestionPro Surveys. AI Router sits behind the conversational survey flow, while custom variables store the router outputs alongside normal survey data. The resulting fields can be used in CX dashboards, filters, workflows, exports and closed-loop action paths.

Core components

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Component                    Role in the solution
  ---------------------------- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  QuestionPro survey flow      Hosts the rating question, conversational prompts and respondent-facing experience.

  AI Router                    Analyzes each answer in real time and determines the next best prompt or analysis output.

  Prompt framework             Defines the adaptive layers: opening, root cause, behavior, trust, future signal and final analysis.

  Custom variables             Store question-level outputs and final structured JSON fields beside the respondent record.

  Structured JSON output       Returns normalized fields such as dominant_theme, secondary_theme, sentiment, experience_stage and improvement_signal.

  Dashboards and workflows     Use the AI fields as filters, segments, routing triggers or operational improvement signals.
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Core conversational flow

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Stage                           How it works
  ------------------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Adaptive opening                The first follow-up changes based on the respondent score, such as detractor, passive or promoter.

  Root cause exploration          The system probes the answer to identify specific drivers, recurring issues, one-time incidents or mixed signals.

  Behavioral impact detection     The conversation explores whether the experience changed customer behavior, effort, usage or future intent.

  Trust and relationship signal   AI Router determines whether confidence, loyalty or perception of the brand has been affected.

  Forward-looking insight         The survey captures a practical improvement signal based on what the customer actually experienced.

  Structured AI analysis          The completed conversation is converted into dashboard-ready fields for reporting and action.
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Structured output model

The final AI Router analysis should return a controlled JSON output so CX teams can analyze conversational feedback at scale instead of reading every open-ended answer manually.

  ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Output field              What it captures
  ------------------------- ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  dominant_theme            The main experience driver behind the response.

  secondary_theme           A supporting theme or contributing driver when one is clearly present.

  sentiment                 A normalized sentiment classification, typically Negative, Neutral or Positive, or an agreed five-point scale.

  experience_stage          The journey stage, touchpoint or interaction area connected to the feedback.

  improvement_signal        The clearest action opportunity, operational fix or reinforcement signal extracted from the conversation.
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When/how a customer would use it

Customers use this when the business needs to understand the why behind scores, not just measure scores. It is strongest when open-ended responses are inconsistent, hard to analyze at scale or disconnected from operational workflows.

The same framework can be used across relationship NPS, post-interaction CSAT, onboarding, digital journeys, support journeys, product feedback, complaint follow-up and closed-loop recovery programs.

5. How do you demo it?

Recommended internal demo flow:

  1. Start with the pain: show a normal rating question followed by a generic open-ended question.

  2. Explain that the score alone does not tell the team what happened, why it happened or what action should be taken.

  3. Launch the AI Router conversational survey and answer as a detractor. Show how the next question adapts to the negative response.

  4. Repeat with a promoter path to show that the same survey does not ask irrelevant complaint-style follow-ups.

  5. Highlight the root cause and behavioral impact layers as the conversation progresses.

  6. Show the final structured AI output with fields such as sentiment, dominant theme, experience stage and improvement signal.

  7. Open the dashboard or reporting view and show how those AI fields become filters, segments or action triggers.

  8. Close by positioning this as a way to turn survey feedback into operational intelligence without creating a long static questionnaire.

Suggested demo talk track

“Most surveys ask for a score and then leave the customer to explain everything in one open text box. AI Router changes that. It listens to what the customer says, asks the next best question, and then converts the conversation into structured fields that the CX team can actually use in dashboards, workflows and follow-up actions.”

9. FAQ

Does this replace traditional surveys?

  No. It enhances traditional surveys by adding an adaptive conversational layer where deeper insight is needed.

Does it increase survey length?

  Not necessarily. It can reduce irrelevant questioning because the survey asks only the next question that makes sense based on the customer’s answer.

How are insights stored and used?

  AI Router structures outputs into predefined fields and stores them alongside response data for filtering, reporting, dashboards and workflows.

Can this work with multilingual feedback?

  Yes. It can process and structure multilingual feedback, but production deployments should include language-specific testing and prompt QA.

Is this real-time?

  Yes. Analysis and question routing happen during the survey experience, not only after data collection is complete.

What are the main implementation risks?

  The main risks are weak prompt design, unclear output fields, too many unnecessary router calls, poor QA of edge cases and dashboard mappings that are not planned before launch.

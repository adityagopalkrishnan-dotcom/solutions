Incidence Rate (IR) Management is a feature within the QuestionPro Audience tool that monitors the real-time incidence rate while the survey is live in the field. It helps keep the survey active and ensures that it receives the required traffic. If there is a sudden drop in the on-field incidence rate or a significant increase in survey length compared to what was estimated at launch, the system tracks these changes and recalculates the cost based on current performance metrics. If a CPI change is required, the system notifies the user and shares the updated metrics along with the suggested CPI at which the survey should be relaunched. How is Incidence Rate(IR) calculated? IR is the percentage of people from the total screened sample who qualify for a study based on the screening criteria. It tells you how common your target audience is in the population you are sampling from. IR is assumed rather than calculated as (number of respondents who will qualify the survey divided by number of respondents who have entered into the survey) multiplied by 100. IR is calculated only based on screening questions, not survey completion behavior. Example: Supose a client requested for 250 responses from people who own an electric car. Total respondents screened = 1000 Completed (Own electric car) = 250 Terminated = 700 Overquota/Dropout = 50 IR is now calculated as = 250(completed)+50(Overquota/Dropout)/ 1000 × 100 So the IR will be 30% What should I do after receiving an email notification from IR Management? When you receive an email notification from IR Management about a live project, it indicates that the survey has been paused due to poor field performance metrics. The notification includes the current on-field IR and LOI, along with the recommended CPI. Step 1: Log in to your account and go to the Audience dashboard. Step 2: Click the Manage button. Step 3: Click Accept to approve the suggested CPI and launch the survey. OR Click on Reject and close to close the project and stop the survey fielding. What should I do if my project is paused due to a critical Incidence Rate? If you do not see an option to relaunch your project or if your project is flagged under Project Critically Paused, it is because your project no longer meets the minimum fielding requirements. A project cannot be relaunched if either of the following conditions is true: The Incidence Rate (IR) is below 10%, or The Length of Interview (LOI) is greater than 20 minutes In such cases, the project is considered not viable for fielding. What should you do? Log in to your account and close the project manually The remaining credits will be refunded to your account Contact support@questionpro.com or your Account Manager before creating or launching a new project What if I don’t have enough credits to accept the suggested CPI? You can add credits to your account using Add more button.
